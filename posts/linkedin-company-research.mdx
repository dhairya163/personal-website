Researching companies on LinkedIn can provide valuable insights that help you tailor your resume for specific job applications. This article provides tips for using LinkedIn to research potential employers and create a targeted resume.

## 1. Follow Company Pages

Follow the LinkedIn pages of companies you're interested in. This allows you to stay updated on their latest news, job openings, and company culture.

## 2. Explore Company Profiles

Visit the LinkedIn profiles of companies to learn about their mission, values, and recent achievements. Pay attention to the content they share and the language they use.

## 3. Review Employee Profiles

Look at the LinkedIn profiles of current employees to understand their backgrounds, skills, and career paths. This can give you insights into the qualifications and experiences the company values.

## 4. Analyze Job Descriptions

Review job descriptions posted by the company to identify key skills, qualifications, and responsibilities. Use this information to tailor your resume for specific job applications.

## 5. Identify Common Keywords

Identify common keywords and phrases used in the company's job descriptions and employee profiles. Incorporate these keywords into your resume to improve its relevance and ATS compatibility.

## 6. Highlight Relevant Experience

Tailor your resume to highlight experiences and achievements that align with the company's needs and values. Use specific examples to demonstrate how your skills match the job requirements.

## 7. Network with Employees

Connect with current or former employees of the company on LinkedIn. Reach out for informational interviews to gain insights into the company culture and hiring process.

## 8. Customize Your Cover Letter

Use the information you've gathered to customize your cover letter for each job application. Mention specific aspects of the company that appeal to you and explain how your skills and experiences make you a good fit.
